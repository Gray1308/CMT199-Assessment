Reflection on Learning

This module taught me mainly the fundamentals of HTML & CSS programming on the technical side, but more importantly than that it also taught me about computation and the problem-solving methods enabled through computational thinking. Prior to this, I had tried my hand at front-end development due to a strong interest in the subject. I had simply implemented a personal blog myself, but later gave up on maintaining it due to other academic pressures. This module has rekindled my interest in the web and has taught me a skill I hadn't mastered before - using selectors! This technique has reduced my development time considerably and the structure of the CSS files is much clearer and easier to adjust and maintain the site structure later on.

In addition to this, the ability to master computational thinking is a huge advantage of this module! As I gradually learned more and more about the world of programming, I realised the importance of computational thinking. Computational thinking is arguably the cornerstone of all programming methods today, and this step-by-step cognitive strategy is very beneficial to my learning at the moment. We all know that JAVA and Python are both object-oriented programming languages and that the abstract concepts of 'classes' and 'inheritance' make it difficult for beginners to understand and master them. But through computer thinking, we can think more critically and creatively. We can also adapt ourselves to the logic of abstract thinking and gradually find real solutions. This knowledge is invaluable for future learning of the flask framework and for future career planning. Through computer thinking, through programming languages, we can create the world we want!

Last but not least, computer thinking has given me the ability to learn by example. That is, the ability to generalise problems and to generalise a class of problems. It helps me to develop the ability to continue learning, to try to solve complex problems from multiple angles and even to come up with new problems. The focus and perseverance I have acquired from this will be the golden key to my future studies and work! Of course it has also taught me the ability to transfer learning, such as cross-disciplinary inspiration, using models from one domain and improving them to solve problems in another domain in a targeted way. These are higher-order competencies that require sweat and luck. Computational thinking can be applied to almost any job and any industry. Having the ability to solve problems, analyse critically, communicate and think creatively, and to go beyond current methods of behaviour in future careers as well as in learning, is priceless in our competitive and ever-changing digital world.
